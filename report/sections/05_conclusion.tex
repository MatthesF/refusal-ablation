\section{Conclusion}

For \texttt{google/gemma-4-E4B-it}, the tested refusal-direction edit causes a
large change on official SORRY-Bench. Baseline compliance is 21.4\%, while
edited compliance is 76.6\%. The paired flip pattern is highly asymmetric:
247 prompts change from refusal to compliance, while only 4 change in the other
direction.

The cleanest conclusion is narrow but strong: in this setup, one fitted
refusal-direction edit removes a large part of benchmark-measured refusal
behavior. The result is statistically clear and practically large, but it should
be interpreted as a benchmark-specific safety-behavior change rather than a full
evaluation of model quality or all safety mechanisms.
